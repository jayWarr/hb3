  1. Terminology

form

has two types of meaning:


A poetic form refers to a distinct poetic construct of a poem e.g. the sonnet poetic form typically has 14 lines. The application has +50 poetic forms that can be used to classify a poem.

The application form refers to the type types of window used to present and manipulate data.


The presentation  listing form is distinguished by having column headings across the top of the form. You can access the appropriate listing form from the appropriate  application menu. Data is presented as a series of records in rows, with each row having a series of fields in columns.  Only data on a listing form can be sorted, filtered or actioned. Data on the listing form cannot be directly created, edited, changed or  deleted. if there are more than 20 records data is initially presented as a series of pages. tt can optionally all  be seen on one page using the show all option. You can select individual records on a listing form (or all of them) for collective action. Every listing form has, in the top right hand corner,  a green  + Add record   button  


which will switch to an empty individual form to create a new record. In addition you can switch from the listing form to the individual form by clicking on a the first, leftmost, field of a row - it will be blue text to view, edit and change data. An individual form presents all the data of a record in two columns. The leftmost column has the identification titles for individual fields. The rightmost column has the corresponding data presented in white boxes; it is this data that can be entered and/or edited. There maybe data presented without a white box - this cannot be edited. Some white boxes, rather than allow data to be typed in directly, present data options as drop down menus. To effect a permanent data entry, or change, you must use either the    Save   or Save and add another entry button. The form also has a History button  which links to a history  form detailing the history of this record.


A Do you want to do this? form appears after you have actioned a deletion. Importantly you are given the opportunity to retract or confirm the deletion(s) before they are carried out.
